Abstract: We investigated the neural correlates of working memory using fMRI. Thirty healthy participants (mean age 24.5, SD 3.2) were scanned at 3T while performing an n-back task. Images were preprocessed using SPM12 and analyzed with a general linear model. Results showed significantly increased activation in the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex during 2-back compared to 0-back (p < 0.001, FWE-corrected). Additionally, working memory performance was positively correlated with dorsal anterior cingulate activation (r = 0.45, p = 0.01).

Abstract: Using diffusion tensor imaging, we examined white matter integrity in patients with schizophrenia (n=25) compared to healthy controls (n=25). Fractional anisotropy was measured in major white matter tracts. We found significantly reduced fractional anisotropy in the corpus callosum (p = 0.003) and uncinate fasciculus (p = 0.012) in patients compared to controls. No significant differences were observed in the cingulum bundle or superior longitudinal fasciculus. Mean diffusivity was elevated in the corpus callosum of patients (p = 0.008).

Abstract: Resting-state fMRI was used to assess default mode network connectivity in 40 individuals with major depression and 40 matched healthy controls. Functional connectivity analysis was performed using seed-based correlation methods. Patients showed reduced connectivity between the posterior cingulate cortex and ventromedial prefrontal cortex (t(78) = 3.42, p < 0.001). This reduction in connectivity was negatively correlated with symptom severity as measured by the Hamilton Depression Rating Scale (r = -0.52, p < 0.001). After antidepressant treatment, connectivity between these regions increased and this increase predicted treatment response (β = 0.38, p = 0.004).

Abstract: We used magnetoencephalography to investigate event-related oscillations during visual attention. Twenty participants viewed visual stimuli while MEG data were recorded. Time-frequency analysis revealed significant alpha-band (8-12 Hz) suppression in occipital regions during attended trials compared to unattended trials (p < 0.01, cluster-corrected). Gamma-band (30-50 Hz) power was enhanced in parietal regions during attention (p = 0.02). No significant differences in theta-band activity were observed between conditions.

Abstract: This study examined the effects of transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS) on motor learning. Participants (n=30) received either anodal tDCS over the primary motor cortex or sham stimulation in a double-blind design. Motor sequence learning was assessed using a finger-tapping task before and after stimulation. Anodal tDCS significantly improved motor learning performance compared to sham stimulation (F(1,28) = 6.84, p = 0.014). The enhancement in learning was correlated with increased corticospinal excitability as measured by motor evoked potentials (r = 0.56, p = 0.002).
