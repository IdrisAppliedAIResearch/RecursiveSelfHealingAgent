Abstract: We investigated the neural correlates of working memory using fMRI. Thirty healthy participants (mean age 24.5, SD 3.2) were scanned at 3T while performing an n-back task. Images were preprocessed using SPM12 and analyzed with a general linear model. Results showed significantly increased activation in the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex during 2-back compared to 0-back (p < 0.001, FWE-corrected). Additionally, working memory performance was positively correlated with dorsal anterior cingulate activation (r = 0.45, p = 0.01).

Abstract: This study examined functional connectivity changes in patients with major depressive disorder. We enrolled 45 MDD patients and 40 healthy controls. Resting-state fMRI data were acquired and analyzed using seed-based correlation analysis. Patients with MDD showed reduced functional connectivity between the subgenual anterior cingulate cortex and the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex compared to healthy controls (p < 0.05, FDR-corrected). No significant group differences were observed in connectivity between the amygdala and hippocampus.

Abstract: We used EEG to investigate event-related potentials during emotional face processing. Twenty participants viewed fearful and neutral faces while EEG was recorded at 500 Hz. ERPs were averaged and analyzed using repeated-measures ANOVA. The N170 component showed greater amplitude for fearful faces compared to neutral faces (F(1,19) = 8.34, p = 0.01). The late positive potential (LPP) was also enhanced for fearful stimuli (p < 0.05). Source localization indicated right occipitotemporal involvement.

Abstract: This study aimed to determine whether structural MRI markers predict cognitive decline in mild cognitive impairment. We scanned 120 MCI patients and 60 controls using 3T MRI. Volumetric analyses were performed using FreeSurfer. At 2-year follow-up, baseline hippocampal volume predicted conversion to Alzheimer's disease (OR = 0.72 per SD decrease, p = 0.003). Entorhinal cortex thinning was also associated with greater cognitive decline (β = -0.31, p = 0.01). No predictive relationship was found between cerebellar volume and cognitive outcomes.
